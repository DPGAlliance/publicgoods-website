Submission Guide

Introduction

In order for your digital solution to be recognized as a digital public good, your solution must conform to the definition of digital public goods. This definition has been translated into a set of indicators together called the DPG Standard.

Requirements to be a DPG

Your project will be reviewed by our technical review team to ensure that it qualifies all requirements of each indicator of the DPG Standard.

The information that you would need to provide in your application form is listed below:

1. SDG RELEVANCE

Your project must demonstrate relevance to one or more SDGs.

Evidence required: A textual description that supplies information that supports the SDG relevance or link(s) to website page, blog article or documentation that adequately demonstrates relevance to one or more of the SDGs.

2. OPEN LICENSING

Your project must use an approved open license.

Evidence required: A public link that explicitly mentions an approved open license. Refer this list of approved open licenses.

3. CLEAR OWNERSHIP

It must be clearly defined that your organization owns the project.

Evidence required: Link(s) to the copyright, trademarks, or publicly available ownership documentation for the project. This could be a page on your website which clearly defines who owns the project. In case there is a parent organization, it should highlight the project ownership on their website. Terms of Service may also indicate the ownership of the project. Other legal documentation that provide supporting information like when the organization was incorporated will strengthen your application.

4. PLATFORM INDEPENDENCE

Your project must not have vendor lock-in. All mandatory dependencies that create more restrictions than the original license should have an open alternative. Non-mandatory dependencies may be replaced with open alternatives without making significant changes to the core product (the source code).

Evidence required: You will need to provide a textual description of how the open-source components are independent and/or list the open alternatives for the closed component. You should be able to demonstrate that close components can be replaced with open alternatives with minimal configuration changes and without requiring a major overhaul of the entire system. This answer should ideally be provided by someone technical on your team.

5. DOCUMENTATION

Your project must be well-documented including the source code, use cases, and/or functional requirements.

Evidence required: A detailed textual description of how the project is documented and links to all relevant documentation such as open repo link, technical documentation, functional requirements, etc depending on the type of project.

For software projects, your documentation should allow a technical person unfamiliar with the project to launch and run the software. For data projects, your documentation should describe all the fields in the set, and provides context on how the data was collected and how it should be interpreted. For content projects, your documentation should indicate any relevant compatible apps, software, or hardware required to access the content and any instructions about how to use it.

For more details on documentation best practices, please visit this link.

6. NON-PII DATA EXTRACTION

If your project collects or uses non-PII (non Personally Identifiable Information) data, then there must be mechanisms for extracting or importing the non-PII data from or into the system in non-proprietary format(s).

Evidence required: A detailed textual description of how the non-PII data can be imported or exported into non-proprietary formats such as CSV/XML/JSON etc or via exposing the non-PII data through APIs.

7. PRIVACY & APPLICABLE LAWS

Your project must comply with relevant privacy, domestic and other applicable international laws.

Evidence required: If your project collects PII (Personally Identifiable Information) data, you will need to provide links to the privacy policy, terms of service or other relevant documentation. Additionally, you may also provide a list of the relevant laws that the project complies with the relevant jurisdiction such (but not restricted to) GDPR, Indian IT Act, Persons with Disabilities Act 2020, Canadian Disabilities Act, Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990.

8. OPEN STANDARDS & BEST PRACTICES

Your project must comply with relevant standards, best practices and/or principles.

Evidence required: A list of the open standards, best practices & principles that your project adheres to. You may provide links where possible as evidence. We have compiled a list of open standards and best practices you may find helpful.

DO NO HARM BY DESIGN

Your project must ensure that steps are taken to anticipate, prevent and do no harm.

Evidence required:

a) If your project collects, stores or distributes PII data, then you will be required to list the types of data that are collected/stored/distributed. And a detailed textual answer that demonstrates how the project ensures the privacy, security and integrity of this data and the steps taken to prevent adverse impacts resulting from its collection, storage and distribution.

b) If your project collects, stores or distributes content, this is valid for you. And a detailed answer that describes the policies for identifying inappropriate and illegal content and the processes for detecting, moderating, reporting and removing inappropriate/ illegal content will strengthen your application. Eg. Child sexual abuse materials.

c) If your project enables interaction between users, you will be expected to provide a textual description demonstrating how the project enables users and contributors to protect themselves from harassment. This could be through a code of conduct.

Application Process

  1. APPLY: We respect your time, so we created a custom application to guide you through the requirements.
  2. REVIEW: After you submit your application, our team will review it in detail against all the indicators of the DPG Standard.
  3. RESULT: If your application passes all the requirements of the DPG Standard, it is certified as a DPG.

FAQs

Who can apply?

Only an authorized representative of the project can submit the application on behalf of the organization(s) or individual(s) that own the project.

Is there a way to check the eligibility of the project before applying?

Yes, please visit our 2-min eligibility check to quickly assess whether your project could meet all the requirements of the DGP Standard.

How much time will it take to review my application?

We try to review all applications within 30 days of submission. But this can go up depending on the volume of applications received. You can read our review policies & process here in more detail.

Where can I track the status of my application?

You can see the status of your application as it goes through our review process on this public GitHub Repo.

How do I increase my chances of acceptance?

Make sure to read and follow the instructions provided within the application form to create a strong application.
