Kindly

Kindly is – an open-source API that detects abusive and cyberbullying intent in messages and provides instant feedback for users to reconsider their message.

Website: https://kindly.unicef.io

Type of Digital Public Good

  • Open content
  • ✅  Open data
  • ✅  Open software
  • Open standard
  • Open AI model

1. Is it relevant to one of the Sustainable Development Goals?

  • 3. Good Health and Well-being

    Evidence: Good health and well-being: According to the UNESCO Institute of Statistics, one third of the globe’s youth is bullied. Bullying affects all youth, including those who are bullied, those who bully others, and those who witness bullying. The effects of bullying may continue into adulthood. Among students ages 12-18 who reported being bullied at school during the school year in the United States, 15% were bullied online or by text. Cyberbullying can affect children mentally, emotionally, and physically. Kindly aims to bring an end to cyberbullying by providing instant feedback to children and giving them the chance to reconsider how they interact with others in order to avoid situations of cyberbullying.

    Link to Evidence: https://unicef.github.io/kindly/#-motivation

  • 4. Quality Education

    Evidence: By providing instant feedback to children on whether a message is considered cyberbullying or not, Kindly tries to educate them so that they can make more informed decisions and avoid situations that can lead to cyberbullying.

    Link to Evidence: https://unicef.github.io/kindly/#-motivation

Was this project built and developed according to or in adherence with any design, technical and/or sector best practices or principles? i.e. the Principles for Digital Development?

Yes

Which principles and best practices does this project support (please list)

  • Child Safeguarding Principles according to UNICEF including:
    1) The wellbeing of the child is the most important consideration - Kindly’s vision is to improve children’s wellbeing by combating cyberbullying;
    2) All children have the same right to be protected, regardless of their gender, culture, ethnicity, age, religion or abilities - Kindly is available to be integrated onto any platform;
    3) Information is managed as confidential - Kindly doesn’t collect any data unless the user wishes to contribute to the training data set in which the data is reviewed and anonymized such that no confidential data is stored permanently or shared.

  • The product’s vision and development also follow the Principles for Digital Development including 1) User-centred design - the perspective of children were taken into account and articulated in the vision through Gitanjali Rao’s involvement and primary user understanding; 2) Design for scale - Kindly’s development involves multiple UNICEF stakeholders and outreach with potential partner organizations so that the solution can be scaled technically and globally; 3) Uses open standards, data, open-source and open innovation - Kindly is developed in the open and uses an open data set from an open source Natural Language processing model, hosted on Huggingface.com called the Twitter-roBERTa-base for Offensive Language Identification; 4) Reuse and improve - As an open-source solution, Kindly’s data set and model can be leveraged by to be included in other solutions. Kindly can also be improved through community contributions to the code and/or datasets; 5) Collaborative - The development of Kindly involves multiple stakeholders throughout the product development process including young innovator, Gitanjali Rao, UNICEF’s Information and Communication Technology Division (ICTD), Division of Communication (DOC), Office of Innovation (OOI), and Child Protection Unit, and external partners including End Violence Against Children (EVAC). (https://digitalprinciples.org/)

  • Other technology best practices include leveraging continuos integration and PEP8 coding style

9. Does the project do no harm by design?

Has this project taken steps to anticipate, prevent and do no harm by design?

On the whole, does this project take steps to ensure that it anticipates, prevents and does no harm by design?

Yes

Is there any additional information you would like to share about the mechanisms, processes or policies that this project uses to avoid doing harm by design?

Even though we do not collect any PII, a user may provide this information by accident while contributing to the training data set. In order to mitigate this risk and avoid any harm to that information, only authorized UNICEF employees will have access to the raw version of the training data. If a user provides any PII by accident, then the authorized team member will safely remove and permanently destroy/delete it prior to including the submission in the public-facing data set.

Through Kindly’s Contribute page, a user can add data to the training data set. A process is in place to ensure that: (1) all the data added by users is reviewed by authorized UNICEF staff; (2) if the user adds data that is personally identifiable, that data is anonymized; (3) this data is initially stored on a secure and encrypted platform that only authorized staff have access to; (4) only after being reviewed, anonymized, and cleaned, the data is made public
