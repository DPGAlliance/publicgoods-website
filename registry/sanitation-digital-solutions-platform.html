Sanitation Digital Solutions Platform

Amref Health Africa in Uganda is partnering with the African Academy of Sciences to pilot a digital sanitation project in Kawempe Division of Kampala district. The project aims at developing a one stop sanitation digital (SaniDigS) platform that is not limited by distance or time.

Website: https://www.sanidigs.org/

Type of Digital Public Good

  • Open content
  • Open data
  • ✅  Open software
  • Open standard
  • Open AI model

1. Is it relevant to one of the Sustainable Development Goals?

  • 3. Good Health and Well-being

    Evidence: With access to better sanitation facilities, there is reduction of water contamination and the related water and sanitation related diseases. We shall see reduction in water and sanitation diseases incidences, reduction in diarrheal related malnutrition and better health outcomes in children under 5 years of age.

  • 6. Clean Water and Sanitation

    Evidence: When people have access to sanitation and hygiene services at a single stop center that is not limited by distance or time, this will improve the turn around time service and access to better sanitation and hygiene and consequently reduce the risks of water contamination thus resulting into inclusive access to sustainable access to safe water, sanitation and hygiene services.

Was this project built and developed according to or in adherence with any design, technical and/or sector best practices or principles? i.e. the Principles for Digital Development?

Yes

Which principles and best practices does this project support (please list)

  • The SaniDigS Platform is built upon the Software Development Life Cycle of Agile development following the six (6) phases:
  • Concept: In this phase we determined the scope of our project and choose Kyebando parish in Kawempe division to pilot our operation. We developed a data gathering plan with the key indicators of the sanitation service chain pointed by Kampala City Council Authority (KCCA) as a performance matrix in order to understand the past sanitation situation, monitor the present and predict the future sanitation of the parish.
  • Inception: Upon the creation of the concept, a development team was assembled to rise to the task upon which workflows and process diagrams we’re produced. Upon each design step, stakeholders were consulted for their input, these included: the Kyebando residents, the sanitation product and service providers, the village leadership, the SaniDigS steering committee, and Amref Health Africa in Uganda project managers.
  • Iteration: The bulk of the SaniDigS platform development was performed in this phase as stakeholders’ input was implemented in the software. App mockups were redrawn, process operations were modified, interface themes and colors were altered to match the vision of the project. All these efforts resulted into a Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
  • Release: With the MVP ready for release, the team proceeded to perform a number of quality assurance tests check functionality and iron out any bugs swiftly whilst documenting these events. Also, during this phase, we trained product and service providers, VHT Champions, and project stakeholders how the system operates mostly in Kyebando at Kyebando Church of Uganda primary school. After the training, the SaniDigS Mobile app for users and the SaniDigS Driver App for product were deployed on Google playstore ready for download and install.
  • Maintenance: Onboarding of clients commenced as performed by the Village Health Team (VHT) Champions. The development team maintains an open line to feedback from these users to maintain stability of the whole system and incorporate new iterations of the system to meet feasible requirements.
  • Retirement: The SaniDigS solution at the moment has not reached this stage as results of performance are just coming in.
