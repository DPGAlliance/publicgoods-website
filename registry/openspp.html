OpenSPP

OpenSPP is an integrated and digital social protection information system that enables governments and humanitarian agencies to streamline the creation and management of assistance programs.

Website: http://openspp.org/

Type of Digital Public Good

  • Open content
  • Open data
  • ✅  Open software
  • Open standard
  • Open AI model

1. Is it relevant to one of the Sustainable Development Goals?

  • 1. No Poverty

    Evidence: OpenSPP enables governments and humanitarian agencies to support the underprivileged through assistance programs. The platform supports both cash transfer and in-kind transfer activities. OpenSPP is currently being deployed in the Middle East to support a country-wide government in-kind transfer program that targets the most vulnerable populations (helping towards the achievement of Target 1.3).

  • 2. Zero Hunger

    Evidence: OpenSPP can be used to distribute food assistance to the most vulnerable populations thanks to offline registration and advance targeting of qualified beneficiaries. MUAC monitoring for mother and child can be implemented to distribute specialized nutritious foods to prevent stunting in children under 5 of age (helping towards the achievement of Target 2.2). OpenSPP is currently being deployed in the Middle East to support a country-wide government in-kind transfer program that targets the most vulnerable populations (helping towards the achievement of Target 2.1).

  • 3. Good Health and Well-being

    Evidence: OpenSPP can be used to implement various types of health and well-being programs, from antenatal and postnatal programs to ensure maternal and child well-being to the distribution of health and hygiene kits. For instance, programs can be configured to implement cash or food distribution in the context of MUAC monitoring for mother and child (helping towards the achievement of Targets 3.1 and 3.2).

  • 4. Quality Education

    Evidence: OpenSPP can be used to implement attendance tracking in school feeding programs that provide both educational and health benefits to the most vulnerable children. School feeding programs act as an incentive for families to enroll and keep children in school (helping towards the achievement of Targets 4.1 and 4.2).

  • 5. Gender Equality

    Evidence: From intent and design to implementation, OpenSPP allows for the integration of gender considerations into all aspects of a social protection program (helping towards the achievement of Target 5.1). The platform can also be used to create and manage school feeding programs that, according to WFP, empower girls by dissuading parents from marrying them off early, which halts their education and can result in child pregnancies.

  • 10. Reduced Inequality

    Evidence: From intent and design to implementation, OpenSPP allows for the integration of inequality considerations into all aspects of a social protection program, helping beneficiaries receive what they need. OpenSPP helps governments implement and monitor social protection policies (helping towards the achievement of Target 10.4).

  • 17. Partnerships to achieve the Goal

    Evidence: OpenSPP is developed by people in the Global South (Thailand, the Philippines, Laos, and Vietnam) for the Global South (helping towards the achievement of Targets 17.6 and 17.9), and it allows and encourages local companies to provide support for the solution. OpenSPP is particularly well suited to provide support to developing countries to significantly increase the availability of high-quality, timely and reliable data disaggregated by income, gender, age, race, ethnicity, migratory status, disability, geographic location and other characteristics relevant in national contexts (helping towards the achievement of Target 17.I).

Yes

If yes - describe the mechanism for extracting or importing non-personally identifiable information from the system in a non-proprietary format:

OpenSPP follows a role-based access control approach. If a user has the access rights to use the import/export feature and to view the data, the system offers the option to import/export it. Three formats are currently implemented: CSV, XML, and JSON.
