OpenLitterMap

OpenLitterMap is an open, interactive, and accessible database of the world's litter and plastic pollution.

Website: https://openlittermap.com/

Type of Digital Public Good

  • Open content
  • Open data
  • ✅  Open software
  • Open standard
  • Open AI model

We collect both personal and non-personal data. If you want to download our data, visit the World Cup page (openlittermap.com/world), scroll down, find a location of your choosing, select the download tab, enter your email, and press submit. If you want to submit data, either download our app, or submit via the website. Alternatively, users may choose to install the APK on their own device directly. Our data is open, anyone can download it in CSV format.

9. Does the project do no harm by design?

Has this project taken steps to anticipate, prevent and do no harm by design?

On the whole, does this project take steps to ensure that it anticipates, prevents and does no harm by design?

Yes

Is there any additional information you would like to share about the mechanisms, processes or policies that this project uses to avoid doing harm by design?

All photos from new users go through a manual verification process to remove any potentially sensitive data (e.g. people could upload pictures of children cleaning beaches). If any embarrassing photos were accidentally uploaded, we screen for and delete these too. We do not capture litter that would increase the risk of already marginalised and vulnerable people, such as human waste, or drug-related paraphernalia. We do have a separate platform for drug-related litter, which local authorities can decide on a level of openness that would be appropriate relative to their local context.

9.c. Protection from harassment

Does this project facilitate interactions with or between users or contributors?

Yes

If yes - does the project take steps to address the safety and security of underage users?

Yes

If yes - please describe the steps this project takes to address risk or prevent access by underage users:

  • Children under the age of 13 should not use our app and produce geographic information. Children between the ages of 13-17 may do so with guardian supervision.

If yes - does the project help users and contributors protect themselves against grief, abuse, and harassment?

Yes

If yes - please describe the steps taken to help users protect themselves.

  • All users are default by anonymous. Users may wish to include social media links, which will appear in the leaderboard or any data they add to the global map. We do not encourage use below the age of 18, but have plans to create resources that would teach people about the responsible use of sharing location data openly online.
