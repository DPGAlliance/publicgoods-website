Digital Public Goods Alliance

Digital technologies have the potential to accelerate sustainable development around the globe and provide financial, educational, and health resources to the world’s most vulnerable populations. Global information goods might include services such as Wikipedia, Open StreetMaps, or Change.org (to name just a few), which cut uniquely across international boundaries, policy silos, and professional domains, and are available to anyone with Internet access.

“The scale, spread and speed of change made possible by digital technologies is unprecedented, but the current means and levels of international cooperation are unequal to the challenge.”

– António Guterres, UN Secretary-General

However, at the moment, there are deep inequalities in access to digital goods.

According to the World Economic Forum, nearly 4 billion people remain disconnected from technology and digital products that could dramatically improve—or even save—their lives.

UNICEF sees this as an opportunity for countries, corporations, NGOs, and individuals to make a concerted effort to
a) connect those without access to this technology, and
b) make digital public goods easily discoverable, accessible, customizable, and usable.
